Marking Scheme
Each question carries 2 marks. Question type is MCQ (Multiple Choice Questions). There is no negative marking — wrong or unanswered questions simply score 0.
  • Since there's no penalty, attempt every single question — never leave one blank.
  • GDS → Postman has 25 extra Postal Knowledge questions compared to GDS → MTS — prioritize Postal Manual / Regulations / Products & Services revision if attempting Postman LDCE.
  • GK and Math weightage (10 + 10 = 20 Qs / 40 marks) is identical across both exams.

Exam Strategy & Tips
  • Postal Knowledge is the highest-weightage subject in both patterns (60% in MTS, ~73% in Postman) — master Postal Manual Vol-V definitions, Vol VI/VII rules, Post Office Regulations 2024, and Products & Services schemes first.
  • GK questions in these LDCEs lean heavily on Static GK (history, geography, polity, books & authors, "firsts in India") plus India Post current affairs — the Postal Study channel's GK series covers this well.
  • Math is basic arithmetic level (10th-class standard) — focus on speed and accuracy on percentage, profit/loss, SI/CI, ratio, time-speed-distance and simplification rather than advanced topics.
  • Since there's no negative marking, attempt 100% of questions and use elimination for unfamiliar ones.
  • Take full-length mock tests under timed conditions (50 min for MTS, 75 min for Postman) to build stamina and pacing.
  • Maintain a Mistake Book — review every wrong answer from your mocks weekly.
